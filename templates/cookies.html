Cookie Settings

We keep it simple — PhishGuard only uses essential cookies. No tracking, no ads, no third-party profiling.

🕐 Last updated: March 2026

1What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored in your browser when you visit a website. They help the site remember things like your session between page loads. PhishGuard uses only the minimum cookies required to function.

✅ PhishGuard uses zero tracking cookies. No Google Analytics, no Facebook Pixel, no advertising cookies of any kind.

2Cookie Categories

🔐
Essential / Session Cookies
Required for the site to function
✓ Always On

These cookies are set automatically by Flask (the web framework powering PhishGuard). They maintain your session as you navigate between pages — for example, ensuring your scan result is displayed correctly after form submission.

These cookies are not optional — disabling them would break core site functionality. They contain no personally identifiable information.

Cookie Name session
Provider PhishGuard (Flask)
Expiry Session (deleted on browser close)
Purpose Session management
🔤
Font & Library CDN Requests
Third-party resource loading (Google Fonts, jsDelivr)
Enabled

PhishGuard loads fonts (Google Fonts) and CSS frameworks (Bootstrap via jsDelivr) from external CDNs. These requests may cause those providers to set their own cookies or log your IP address as part of their standard operation.

We have no control over these third-party cookies. If you're concerned, you can disable this category — the site will still work but may appear with fallback system fonts.

Providers fonts.googleapis.com, jsDelivr
Purpose Typography, UI framework
Tracking? None by PhishGuard
📊
Analytics & Tracking Cookies
Behavioral tracking, advertising
Disabled

PhishGuard does not use any analytics, advertising, or behavioral tracking cookies — and never will. This category is shown here only for full transparency. There is nothing to enable.

Google Analytics · Facebook Pixel · Hotjar · Ad networks — None of these are used.
